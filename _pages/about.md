Hello! 

I am a health economist and postdoctoral fellow in health policy and insurance research at the Harvard Pilgrim Health Care Institute. I also hold a secondary appointment at RAND. My research spans topics in behavioral health care, health care financing, workforce development, and population health.

My work has appeared in or received requests for revisions at leading academic journals, such as the _Journal of Public Economics_, _Journal of Health Economics_, _Journal of Policy Analysis and Management_, _Pediatrics_, and _American Journal of Epidemiology_. 

My dissertation examines how recent U.S. policy changes affect health and economic outcomes for low-income and vulnerable populations. The first chapter studies the health effects of recent and historically large minimum wage hikes, showing null or small negative consequences for birth outcomes and female employment. The second chapter assesses the regulation of pain management clinics, a quasi-legal market in behavioral health care, and finds that increases in regulatory oversight over providers can temper inappropriate prescribing and direct dispensing, while leading to decreases in overdose mortality from opioid analgesics. The third chapter evaluates Medicaid work requirements and finds that conditioning health insurance on employment does not increase labor market engagement, but rather decreases Medicaid participation through both direct disenrollment of existing beneficiaries and decreases in Medicaid uptake among the broader population.

I received my Ph.D. in policy analysis with a concentration in economics from the RAND School of Public Policy. Please feel free to reach out at ymizushi[at]rand.org with any questions or collaboration interests.
